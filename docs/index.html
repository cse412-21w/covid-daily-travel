TRAFFIC

The highly infectious disease COVID-19 pandemic has caused unprecedented live and economical loss in the United States, also poses a significant challenge for us. In different parts of the country, different types of people are doing different things that changed the pandemic in different scales, significantly or not, our behaviors are interfering with this world.

Since mid-February 2020, states were, more or less, affected by coronavirus. These distinctions might potentially result from travel behaviors, population, unemployment rate, age groups etc., and they all somewhat interfered the disease spreading processes. Macroscopically, those states with high number of confirmed cases actually have relatively low infection rate (≈ 7.5%) while most of the states with high infection (apprx 11%) have relatively smaller number of confirmed cases. More analyses related to aforementioned factors would be introduced later.

The color represents the severity of infection rate of each state and the size of bubble represents the state‚Äôs total number of confirmed cases. In other words, states with darker color have higher infection rate and states with larger size have more confirmed cases.

In order to better understand the impacts of COVID-19 on different states, 4 metrics were applied:

  - Stay-Home Rate: Population of Staying at home / Total Population
  - Stay-Home Density: Population of Staying at home / State Area in sq.mi
  - Unemployment Change: Difference of Unemployment Rate between 2019 and 2020
  - Total Trips: Total Number of trips in 2020 after February

In general, most of the states had an approximately +10% leap for Stay-Home Rate at mid-March, then there was a sudden 10% decrease in May. It‚Äôs interesting that there were not any big events or policy enforcement in May that might lead to such sudden reduction. Afterwards, the rate gradually reached to 10% increase in December comparing to May.

For all states, the 2020 unemployment rate substantially increased in April comparing to 2019 and gradually approximate to the level of 2019 afterwards. Moreover, the Stay-Home Density and Total Trips most of the states did not vary a lot after February in 2020 except for California.

To change a metric, it only needs to click on the category bar. To change a state, it takes 2 steps: select a state and a category.

Category

Yes, people were still travelling, but for very different distances. Researches showed that travel distances are highly related to the infection rate. During the pandemic, people tend to travel shorter distance. Although the overall travel distance had declined a lot because of the pandemic, traveling of various distances remain existing. The interesting thing was that while shorter distance travel number declined over time, longer distance travel number remained at a relatively constant level.

The travel distance can be categorized into 4 types of travel ranges: walkable trips [0 mile, 3 miles), commute trips [3 mile, 25 miles), outdoor activity trips [25 miles, 100 miles), travelling trips [100 miles, +∞). Shown in the graph are the number of each type of travel range over the country, along with the number of each types of travel every month. The time period and travel distance type can be chosen by users.

People were travelling, but different age groups travels differently, and people‚Äôs travel patterns are very different between a normal year and a year with pandemic. When we put them side by side, it became more obvious that the overall travels had declined a lot in the past year. Adults, adolescence and senior adults had a declination in all types of travel ranges; however, the declination was not as dramatical as those of the Child. A possible explanation for this was that the stay home order affected children‚Äôs lives the most.

People are categorized into 4 age groups: Child age [0, 12], Adolescence age (12, 18], Adult age (18, 59], Senior Adult age (60, +∞]. Users can select different age groups to view the result.

To summarize, total trips for most of the states did not vary a lot. Among the 4 types of trips, the number of trips with shorter distance declined a lot while trips with longer distance remained at a relatively constant level. In addition, travel preferences for states with different percentage of the 4 age groups had a similar declination in all types of travel ranges except for states with high percentage of child.
